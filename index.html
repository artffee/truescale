How it works

Three steps to a true-size measurement

A CSS pixel isn't a fixed physical length — it changes with every screen. Calibration is what turns this from a rough guide into a real ruler.

Calibrate with any card

Open calibration and resize the outline until a real credit, debit, or ID card matches it exactly. Every such card is 85.60 mm wide, so this fixes your screen's true scale. On phones, match the card's short edge instead.

Place your object on the ruler

Hold the object flat against the screen, lined up with the scale. The ticks are redrawn from your calibration, so they're physically correct at any zoom or orientation.

Drag the caliper jaws

Slide markers A and B to the edges of your object. The live readout shows the distance in your chosen unit, with the other units beneath it.

Why calibrate

The pixel problem

Browsers assume a notional 96 pixels per inch, but the real size of a pixel depends on your display's density and device-pixel-ratio. A naïve ruler drawn at 96px = 1in is wrong on most phones and many monitors.

TrueScale solves this with one number — pxPerMm, the CSS pixels that equal one real millimetre on your display. Match the card once and that value drives every tick and every reading. It's stored in your browser, so returning visitors keep their calibration.

FAQ

Common questions

How accurate is an on-screen ruler?
After calibrating with a standard card, TrueScale is accurate to roughly ±1 mm — tighter than the eye can judge against a screen. Without calibration it falls back to a 96 PPI estimate and is clearly flagged as approximate.
Do I need a credit card to calibrate?
Any ISO/IEC 7810 ID-1 card works — credit, debit, ID, membership, or transit cards are all exactly 85.60 mm wide. You can also enter your screen's PPI directly if you know it.
Does it work on phones?
Yes. A full card is wider than most phone screens, so on a narrow display switch calibration to the card's short edge (53.98 mm), which fits. Drag, touch, and pen input are all supported, and the ruler redraws correctly when you rotate.
Is my data collected?
No personal data is collected and nothing you measure is uploaded. Your calibration is saved only in your own browser's local storage and never sent anywhere.
Will my calibration be remembered?
Yes. It's stored locally and reused on every visit. If TrueScale detects you may have switched device or display, it will gently suggest re-calibrating — and you can re-open calibration any time.
